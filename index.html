HOME COOK APPLICATION

Fullstack MEAN application

What do developers not only want but also definitely need? Or anybody else working in any other fast-paced hi-techwork environment? Or other busy working parents? Food, of course. Delicious, healthy and nutritiousfood that they did not had to prepare themselves because it is just too time-consuming.And how can we help stay-at-home parents, grandparents, new immigrants or even refugees earn extra money to help support their families? My app proposes a win-win situation by connecting these two seemingly unrelated populations. Thanks to Airbnb many people are more than happy to sleep in a stranger’s apartment. And with Uber you pay someone to be your personal chauffeur. So isn’t it time for an app that connects you to people who want to sell you a home-cooked meal?

Hungry customers of “The Home Cook app” use a google map to find the nearest home cooks and to see what is on the menu. Once they have selected a certain area they see:

  • A photo of the home Coook
  • Mouth-watering photos of the food
  • A small description of the food. For example,if it is vegan,kosher or has certain ingredients such as peanuts or gluten that may cause allergies
  • Ratings and reviews from previous users
  • Availability of the menu

Home Coook is a MEAN application We used a Google maps API to show the nearest home cooks to your current position, for the frontend we used Angular and UI router, and in the backend we used Mongodb, Express and Nodejs and for Authorization we used sessions. To go to the website click Here

BEER LIST

Fullstack MEAN application

Beginning an application from scratch can sometimes be the hardest thing to do. Staring at an empty folder and a file with no code in it yet can be a very daunting thing. This app was my starting point to learned how to begin a MEAN stack application. I was able to start from absolute scratch and create a basic application structure that allow me to build any sort of application I want.

Users of “The beer list app” can sign up and be able to modify the data inside of the app or not login and just see the information and the reviews of each beer. Once they login they can see and do:

  • Information about the beers such a name, alcohol by volume, style and a picture.
  • Modify the name, alcohol by volume and the beer style just clicking on it.
  • Add a rating and can see the average rating and delete the beer
  • Add a review and see all the reviews from different users
  • Delete the reviews and sign out

Mi Casa

Second place winning in Davivienda Hackathon 2016

During this unprecedented 24-hour event, hosted by SIT - Innovation Consulting, our team got to work 1-on-1 with a team of four experts from Davivienda Bank on coming up with our idea and adapting our solutions to the bank's problem. My team worked closely for two days with the bank's management on solidifying their business case and technological viability. One intense workday to get halfway through the sales funnel - in an event that was also incredibly fun.

MiCasa app main objective in this hackathon was to develop products and services that strengthened the connection between Davivienda and Colombians living and staying abroad, with an emphasis on migrant workers.
